Vehicle Summary

The new SL-Class is a reconceptualized icon. Never before had Mercedes' flagship convertible been developed straight out of the AMG division. This very fact has several implications for the once-plush roadster, the most significant being that sportiness now takes precedence over outright luxury.

Overview

The Mercedes-AMG SL55 offers a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V-8 engine making 469 hp and 516 lb-ft of torque. The top-spec SL63 churns out 577 hp and 590 lb-ft of torque because of higher boost pressure, improved internal airflow, and a software upgrade. The result is a 0-60 mph time of 3.1 seconds, according to our tests. A nine-speed automatic transmission sends power to an all-wheel-drive system in both SL variants. The EPA rates the SL55 at 13/21 mpg city/highway while the SL63 takes a slight hit due to its extra power at 14/22 mpg.

Safety

Mercedes equips the SL-Class with a variety of active driver assistance features. Automated emergency braking with pedestrian detection, lane departure warning with lane keeping assist, blind-spot monitoring, active LED headlights, high-beam assist, and a 360-degree camera system are included. Forward pre-collision avoidance with cross-traffic monitoring, speed limit assist, blind-spot assist, and adaptive cruise control with a semi-autonomous driving mode are available.
