Vehicle Summary

The new 2.4-liter flat-four engine produces 228 hp and 184 lb-ft of torque—or so Toyota claims. Compare that to the 2020 Toyota 86, which was good for 205 hp at 7,000 rpm and just 156 lb-ft of torque at 6,400 rpm. That was with a manual; the automatic numbers came to 200 hp and 151 lb-ft.

The 2022 GR 86 will be available with a six-speed manual or six-speed automatic transmission, sent to—of course—the rear wheels. EPA-rated fuel economy lists at 21/31 mpg city/highway with the automatic, and 20/27 with the manual.

Overview

Officially called the GR86, Toyota's new rear-wheel-drive sports car enters its second generation with something enthusiasts have waited for since the first model came out years ago: more power. Moving from a 2.0-liter to a 2.4-liter I-4 engine means the new 2022 Toyota GR86 is quicker than before, and is ready to challenge other budget-friendly fun cars like the Subaru BRZ with which it shares a platform, the Ford Mustang, and even the Volkswagen GTI.

Safety

Go with an automatic-transmission model and the 2022 86 will come with Subaru-sourced EyeSight active safety and convenience tech. That safety suite will include automatic emergency front braking, adaptive cruise control, lane departure warning, high beam assist, and lead vehicle start alert, which notifies the driver when the vehicle ahead has moved up in traffic.
